Brief introduction

Modern Sichuan cuisine has risen in the Qing Dynasty and the Republic of China, and has been innovated after the founding of New China. Sichuan cuisine is mainly home-cooked, supplemented by high-end dishes. The ingredients are mostly daily, and there is no shortage of Shanzhen seafood. It is characterized by red taste, spicy, fresh and fragrant; white taste is varied, including sweet, halogen, and strange flavors. On behalf of the dishes are fish-flavored pork, Kung Pao chicken, boiled fish, boiled pork, husband and wife lung slices, spicy chicken, Mapo tofu, pork back, Dongpo elbow and Dongpo meat, etc. Other classic dishes are: Stick chicken, pickled chicken feet, light shadow beef, Liao ribs, saliva chicken, spicy shrimp, pepper fried beef, Sichuan hot pot, spicy perfume fish, chestnut roast chicken, spicy chicken and so on.
