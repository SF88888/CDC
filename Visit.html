Weather

Chengdu has a subtropical monsoon climate with the characteristics of spring, summer, autumn and winter warm. The annual average temperature is 16 °C and the annual rainfall is about 1000 mm. A notable feature of Chengdu's climate is cloudy fog and short sunshine hours. The “song of the dog” in the folk proverb is the image description of this climatic feature. Another remarkable feature of Chengdu's climate is that the air is humid. Therefore, although the temperature is not high in summer (the highest temperature generally does not exceed 35 °C), it is sultry; in winter, the average temperature is above 5 °C, but due to the cloudy days, the air tide, but It looks very cold. The rainwater in Chengdu is concentrated in July and August, and it is dry and rainy in winter and spring, with little snow and ice.

The extreme minimum temperature in Chengdu is -5.9 °C, most of the districts and counties appear in December, and a small number appear in January. Chengdu is a subtropical humid monsoon climate zone. The most common wind direction in Chengdu is calm wind. The second wind direction: north wind in June, July and August, and northerly wind in the rest of the month.

Airport & Train station

Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport is the resident of Southwest Civil Aviation Administration and Southwest Air Traffic Control Bureau.

Chengdu Airport is the base airport of China International Airlines, Sichuan Airlines, Chengdu Airlines, China Eastern Airlines, Tibet Airlines and Xiangpeng Airlines.

With the approval of the State Council, from January 1, 2019, Chengdu will implement the 144-hour transit visa exemption policy for foreigners. Persons from 53 countries will have valid international travel documents and a fixed date within 144 hours. ) For the joint ticket, you can get a visa-free entry from the relevant port and stay in Chengdu for 144 hours.
